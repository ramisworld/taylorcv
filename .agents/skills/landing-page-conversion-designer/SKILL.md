# Landing Page Conversion Designer

Use this skill for landing page and marketing website work.

## Goal

Make TaylorCV instantly understandable, trustworthy, and conversion-focused.

The user should understand within seconds:

- What TaylorCV does
- Who it is for
- Why it is better than a generic CV builder
- What to click next

## Positioning

TaylorCV turns any job ad into a tailored, polished, one-page CV for that role.

Core value:

- Extracts important job requirements
- Matches them to the candidate’s background
- Finds missing evidence
- Asks a few smart gap questions
- Generates a stronger tailored CV

## Primary CTA

Default CTA:

- “Build my tailored CV”

CTA rules:

- One obvious primary CTA per section.
- CTA should be visible above the fold.
- Avoid multiple competing primary actions.
- Secondary CTA can exist, but must be visually quieter.

## Trust rules

Do not invent:

- User counts
- Interview improvement percentages
- Company logos
- Named testimonials
- Case studies
- “Trusted by Microsoft/Deloitte/etc.”
- Guaranteed outcomes

Use believable early-stage proof:

- Built for New Zealand job seekers
- ATS-safe formatting
- One-page tailored CV
- Role-aware structure
- Evidence-backed bullet improvements
- Designed for students, graduates, tradespeople, and professionals

## High-converting sections

Prefer sections that show product value directly:

- Hero with product preview
- Job ad → requirements → evidence match → tailored CV transformation
- Before/after CV bullet comparison
- Match score improvement explanation
- Smart gap questions preview
- ATS-safe one-page CV preview
- Objection handling before pricing
- Simple pricing with clear value

Avoid generic sections:

- “Features” grids with vague cards
- Fake testimonials
- Logo clouds without proof
- Long mission statements
- Decorative AI sections that do not explain value

## Visual rules

Hero:

- Must show the product, not just abstract graphics.
- Use a crisp CV preview.
- Show role match or requirements extraction.
- Make CTA dominant.

Before/after:

- Show specific improvement.
- Use realistic CV bullet examples.
- Show why the tailored version is better.

Proof:

- Use real proof only.
- If no real proof exists, use credibility based on product mechanics and specificity.

## Copy rules

Prefer:

- Specific
- Believable
- Outcome-oriented
- Simple
- Direct

Avoid:

- Hype
- Vague AI language
- Fake urgency
- Inflated claims

## Review checklist

Before finishing, check:

- Can a first-time visitor explain TaylorCV after 5 seconds?
- Is the CTA obvious?
- Is the page specific to New Zealand job seekers?
- Does the page show the product workflow?
- Is there any fake proof?
- Is the value stronger than “ChatGPT can rewrite my CV”?
- Does every section increase trust or clarity?
